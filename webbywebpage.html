Overview

Well, if you're reading this you probably thought becoming a builder was a fantastic idea. Well you're wrong. Its a terrible idea. We're here to tell you exactly why that is. If you've been harbouring thoughts of big bucks, horny housewives, tax rebates and a general Life of Riley, we are here to put those myths to bed once and for all. Read on for more insights into the cess pit of sheer idiocy that the building trade has become and, why you should avoid getting involved at all costs...

So, what exactly is going on with the building trade?

Everybody seems to think that builders make $$$$$$$ but that really isnt the case at all. Think about it, you have to go to the job at least twice before you even get it, once to look at it and get an idea, once to have a proper look to get all the bits you forgot to look at last time you were there, because you're a numpty. Then you've gotta 'get the gear'.

This usually involves a 'McBreakfast' of some description because you were too lazy to cook any proper breakfast that morning, then, when you get to the merchants, you realise that since covid, every merchant and his dog has decided they can really take the mickey and have doubled the cost of everything.

Absolutely everything.

Even stuff that has no business going up in price like those little pencils they have on the counter with 'acme builders merchants' engraved on em that snap the first time you use em.
Its just not worth it, trust me.
You then realise you've gone and underquoted the job so you drive over to the punters house feeling like a bag of crap cos you know you're gonna make sod all out of this job.
Then the punter starts moaning that 'well you said you were gonna do x,y and z' on top of what you've quoted for. Well you can't remember cos you'd had a couple of beers before you went round and gave em the quote, and you know what you're like when you've had a few.. get all charitable and that so now you gotta do all that too.. for free.. numpty.
Telling you, its just not worth it. Dont do it. Simples. It's not just since covid that this has been going on.
This absolute pigs ear of a calamity of a business model has been brewing for the last 40 years. Ever since Maggie 'the milk snatcher' Thatcher kicked the miners front door in and threatened to cut off their winkles if they dared to ask for a pay rise, the entire construction industry has been absolutely shitting itself.

Back in the 90's we had the 'White Gold' rush where everyone was running round shoving cheap plastic windows in every hole they could find but even those days are sadly behind us.
You could jump on the 'Air Source Heat Pump (sponsored by )bandwagon but good luck with that one, the bloody things just dont work. At all. COP 3? My arse. The only way you'll get a COP of 3 is in the middle of summer when you dont actually need the bloody thing. In the dead of winter, when you DO actually need it that Ace No.1 performance rating drops like a pisshead in a boxing match. It's cheaper to burn your life savings in the hearth.
The problem is, nobody has had a pay rise in the building industry since Yosser Hughes was last heard shouting "Gis a Job!" on every building site in the north and thats largely down to Tory policy.
It goes something like this:

1. Allocate 0.5% annual budget to building more houses.
2. Give large lucrative contracts to your mates.
3. These 'friends of the prime minister' then go find the local tory councillor and offer em a slice in return for a kickback.

Said councillor then offers up some kind of 'Development Grant' to local landlords who he's a bit pally with (b'cos he's got a few rentals himself) and these guys go looking for some mug to clean the mould off the bedsit walls some single mother is trying to bring a kid up in. You then come along with your polished looking 'Schedule of works', he gives it to all the other numpties telling them 'Beat that quote and its yours mate', until finally the job gets done by Sid the dosser for 20 quid and a bag of chips.
(Sid doesn't care, he's got his dole money, his PIP and he doesnt have to pay tax on a cash job).

This is gonna be your life.
Quoting against the lowest bidder, thinking the standard of your work makes you 'stand out from the crowd'. Don't kid yourself...
